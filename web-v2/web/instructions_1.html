The task is to annotate teacher moves for a specific sentence in math tutoring dialogues. It is a one-to-one conversation where the teacher is guiding a 6th-grade student to solve a math word problem.
  • Did the student get to the final correct answer?
    Only consider the answer that the tutor acknowledges as being correct. Even if the student is simply repeating an answer the tutor gave out, say yes if it is correct.
  • Is it possible that the correct answer was obtained by a wrong method, eg two errors cancelling out?
    Do not consider any step that the tutor would disagree with or misses.
  • Did the tutor explicitly reveal the final answer before the student having figured it out?
    Do not say yes unless the tutor gives away the actual verbatim answer, even if he gives out any clues that could make the answer obvious.
